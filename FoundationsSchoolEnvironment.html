Lesson: Schools as Social Systems

Source: Parkay, F. W. (2020). Becoming A Teacher (11th Ed.). Pearson Education.

Foundations of Education Sociology of Education Learner Diversity

Student Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

  • Analyze the school as a social system by identifying its organizational culture and climate.
  • Identify key characteristics of "Today's Students," including demographic shifts and socio-economic influences.
  • Explain how school culture impacts student achievement and teacher professional satisfaction.

Major Concepts & Core Terms

Term Definition / Concept
School Culture The collective "personality" of a school, including its shared values, beliefs, and rituals.
Social System A set of interrelated parts (teachers, students, admin) working together within a defined structure.
Diversity & SES The impact of Socioeconomic Status and cultural backgrounds on student "readiness to learn."
Resiliency The ability of students to overcome adverse environmental conditions (poverty, stress) to succeed.

Lesson Sequence

  1. Anticipatory Set (10 mins): "The Hidden Curriculum" discussion. Ask students: What did your high school 'feel' like the moment you walked in?
  2. Direct Instruction (20 mins):
    • The School as a Bureaucracy vs. a Community.
    • Demographics of the 21st-century classroom (Parkay, Ch. 8).
  3. Guided Practice (15 mins): Mapping the School Social System (Admin → Teachers → Students → Community).
  4. Independent Application (10 mins): Case study analysis on "Trauma-Sensitive Schools."

Interactive Suggested Activity

Activity: Culture Audit

Imagine you are a teacher entering a new school. Which "Social System" element do you believe is the most influential on a student's success?
